§27. (3rd Declension.—Masculines and Feminines that form the Nominative Singular without adding -s.)

(A). C. Julius Caesar was a famous (clarus, §15) general of the Romans in the first century B.C. Great was the glory (§4) of C. Julius Caesar. The Gauls feared (§2; say used-to-fear) Caesar. For within (§22) three years he had defeated (§24) the Helvetii1 in South Gaul and the Veneti on the Gallic coast and the tribes of Belgic Gaul. There-were many Caesars before and after C. Julius Caesar. The Romans used-to-name2 the Caesars 'Generals.' The forces of the Caesars were great. My uncle has told me many-things about the Caesars.

(B). Why did Caesar wage-war against Britain? The cause (§9) of the expedition against Britain is known (§16). During the war with the Veneti some of the tribes3 of Britain had supplied auxiliaries to4 the Veneti. For the Veneti had been friends of the tribes of South Britain during many years. There had also been war between the tribes of South Britain. And the Trinobantes were friends of the Romans. Accordingly (§19) Caesar prepared to supply aid to the Trinobantes against the Cassi.

1 Helvetii, the plural of Helvetius, is a noun of the 2nd Declension. The Helvetii lived in Helvetia (Switzerland).

2 Use nomino 'I name,' from which comes the Adjective nominatus, a, um 'named' (§11).

3 Translate 'tribe' by natio (instead of populus) in this and all following exercises.

4 The verb 'to supply' is a verb of 'giving.' How, then, is to the Veneti to be translated?

§28. (Same Nouns continued.)

Caesar's first expedition was not great; but in the second expedition of the next year a great multitude of vessels and five legions sailed with Caesar to Britain. How-many5 men were-there in a Roman legion? How-many men were-there in five Roman legions? The number was different (§19) in different centuries. Among6 Caesar's forces were also many Gallic auxiliaries. For the Belgae and other Gallic tribes had supplied forces to Caesar. Many Caesars were warlike. In the first century A.D. one (§22) of the Caesars named Claudius was the second conqueror (victor) of Britain. I do not love the Caesars; but C. Julius Caesar was a great man and a great general.

5 'How-many' is quot (indeclinable; see Latin Drill §13).
