Third Declension—continued.
NOUNS LIKE 'PAX,' 'AESTAS,' etc.

A great many nouns of the 3rd Declension differ from those already learned by adding an s to the Nominative Singular; thus from the stem 'pāc-' we get the Nominative Singular 'pāc-s' (written with the letter x for cs); but the other cases are formed from the stem 'pāc-.' When the stem ends in a t (or d), this letter disappears in the Nominative Singular when the s is added, but not in the other cases. Thus we decline—

1, 2 pāx aestā-s mīle-s virtū-s
3 pāc-em aestāt-em mīlit-em virtūt-em
4 pāc-is aestāt-is mīlit-is virtūt-is
etc. etc. etc. etc.

Nouns of the 3rd Declension that form the Nominative Singular by adding s to the stem are mostly feminine, except those which denote male persons, like 'mīles,' soldier, and a few others.
